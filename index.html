ShÅ«ji Tsushima, known by his pen name Osamu Dazai, was a Japanese novelist and author.

Here's a time line of Osamu Dazai's life:

  • 1909 - Born June 19, Kanagi, Aomori prefecture, 6th son of Tane and Genemon Tsushima; one of the richest landlords in Aomori.
  • 1930 - Enters Tokyo University. Attempts a double suicide with Shimeko Tabe, November 28.
  • 1931 - Lives with Hatsuyo Koyama in Tokyo, Feburuary.
  • 1933 - First publishing work Gyofukuki in Kaihyo, March; Omoide in Kaihyo, June and July.
  • 1935 - Attempts suicide in Kamakura, March.
  • 1936 - Bannen published, June 26.
  • 1937 - Attempts a double suicide with Hatsuyo Oyama in Tanigawa Spa, April.
  • 1939 - Marriage of Michiko Ishihara, Fugaku Hyakkei in Buntai, February and March.
  • 1944 - Tsugaru published, November.
  • 1945 - Moves to Kanagi, refuge from Tokyo Air Raid; Otogizoshi published, October.
  • 1946 - Returns to Mitaka, Tokyo.
  • 1947 - Villon's Wife published, July. The Setting Sun published, November.
  • 1948 - Completes No Longer Human , May. Commits a double suicide with Tomie Yamazaki, July 13; found June 19, same day of his birth.
